# TASK-3-Causality-Assessment-of-Drug-Reactions

# Objective
To evaluate the relationship between a suspected drug and an adverse reaction using standard pharmacovigilance causality assessment principles.
# Case Summary
- Patient: 58-year-old Male

- Suspected Drug: Ciprofloxacin

- Adverse Reaction: Diarrhea

- Onset: 2 days after starting Ciprofloxacin

- Outcome: Recovered after stopping the drug

# Causality Assessment
# 1. Temporal Relationship

Assessment: Positive

Diarrhea developed two days after initiation of Ciprofloxacin, demonstrating a reasonable temporal relationship between drug administration and the adverse event.

# 2. Dechallenge

Assessment: Positive

The diarrhea resolved after discontinuation of Ciprofloxacin, indicating a positive dechallenge.

# 3. Alternative Causes

Assessment: No alternative cause identified

No concurrent illness, infection, or other medication was reported that could account for the adverse reaction.

# 4. Rechallenge

Assessment: Not Performed

The patient was not re-exposed to Ciprofloxacin; therefore, rechallenge information is unavailable.
# Final Classification

Causality Category: Probable

# Justification
The adverse reaction appeared after the administration of Ciprofloxacin, improved following withdrawal of the suspected drug, and no alternative explanation was identified. Since rechallenge was not performed, the available evidence supports a Probable causal relationship.
# Learning Outcome
This exercise highlights the importance of evaluating temporal association, dechallenge, alternative causes, and rechallenge when determining whether a drug is responsible for an adverse reaction. Systematic causality assessment is an essential part of pharmacovigilance and contributes to improved patient safety.
